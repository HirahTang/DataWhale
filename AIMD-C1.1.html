Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a field of study that focuses on the creation of intelligent agents, which are systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. The foundation of AI includes natural language processing, knowledge representation, automated reasoning, and machine learning.

The history of AI can be traced back to the 1950s, when Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test as a measure of a machine's ability to demonstrate human-like intelligence. Today, AI has advanced significantly, with the state-of-the-art (SOTA) achieving impressive results in areas such as computer vision and robotics. However, the development and deployment of AI also brings with it risks and benefits that must be carefully considered.

To understand human thought, researchers have also studied introspection, conducted psychological experiments, and used brain imaging techniques. The ultimate goal of AI is to align the objectives of machines with the real objectives of humans, a concept known as "value alignment."
